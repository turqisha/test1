HTML 2.0 was published as RFC 1866. Supplemental RFCs added capabilities
  • November 25, 1995: RFC 1867 (form-based file upload}
  • May 1996: RFC 1942 (tables)
    1. August 1996: RFC 1980 (client-side image maps)
    2. January 1997: RFC 2070 (internationalization)

HTML 3

January 14, 1997
HTML 3.2 was published as a W3C_Recommendation It was the first version developed and standardized esclusively by the W3C,
as the UETF had closed its HTML working group on september 12, 1996. Initialy code-named "Wilbur",HTML 3.2 dropped math
formulas entirely, reconciled overlap among various proprietary extensions and adopted most of Netscape's visual markup tags. Netscape's blink
edelent and Microsoft's (6) marquee element were omitted due to a mutual agreement between the two companies. A markup for mathematical formulas
similar to that of HTML was standardized 14 months later in Mathml.
2012 HTML5 0 Candidate W3C_Recommendation 1

In July 2012, WHATWG and W3C decided on a dagree of separation. W3C will continue the HTML5 specification work,
focusing on a single definitive standart, which is considered a "snapshot" by WHATWG. The WHATWG organization will
continiue its work with HTML5 as a "Living standart".
2014 HTML- Proposed Recommendation and Recommendation 2

In September 2014, W3C moved HTML5 to Proposed Recommendation. On 28 October 2014, HTML5 was released as a stable W3C
Recommendation, meaning the specification process is complete.
